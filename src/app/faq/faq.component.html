Frequently Asked Questions

chevron_rightWhat is LNG?

LNG is an acronym for liquefied natural gas.

In short, LNG is natural gas cooled to -162 degrees Celsius which condenses it into a liquid form. After it has been liquefied, it takes up much less space â€“ approximately 1/600th less than it would in its natural form. This makes it easier and safer to transport.

Once condensed, LNG can be loaded on to cargo ships built for overseas transport. After reaching its end destination, it is re-converted to natural gas and processed for market use.

chevron_rightWhat is LNG Canada?
LNG Canada is a large-scale natural gas processing and export facility located in Kitimat, British Columbia. After natural gas is converted into a liquid form it will be shipped to Asia.
chevron_rightWhat is the Coastal GasLink pipeline?
Coastal GasLink is a 670-kilometer pipeline that will supply the LNG Canada facility with raw natural gas from northeastern British Columbia.
